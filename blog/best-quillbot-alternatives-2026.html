Best QuillBot Alternatives in 2026 — AI Paraphrasing & Rewriting Tools

QuillBot is the go-to AI paraphraser, but is it the best? We compare QuillBot vs Grammarly, Jasper, Writesonic, and 4 more writing AI tools in 2026.

6 tools reviewed Updated 2026-04-18

QuillBot built its reputation on paraphrasing — paste text, choose a mode (Standard, Fluency, Formal), get rewritten output. It works. But in 2026, every major AI writing tool paraphrases, and many do it with more context-awareness, longer outputs, and deeper grammar fixing.

We tested QuillBot, Grammarly, Jasper, Writesonic, Rytr, and Copy.ai side-by-side on academic, marketing, and business copy. Here's what actually won.

1

QuillBot

Best-in-class paraphraser with 8 modes (Standard, Fluency, Formal, Academic, Creative, Simple, Expand, Shorten). Integrates with Chrome and MS Word. Best for students and academics.

Free / $9.95/mo Visit QuillBot →
2

Grammarly

Goes beyond paraphrasing — it rewrites with awareness of tone, brand voice, and audience. AI writing suggestions in every app. Best for professionals who want a writing coach, not just a reworder.

Free / $12/mo Visit Grammarly →
3

Jasper

Long-form AI writing built for marketers. Brand voice training, SEO integration, and templates for 50+ content types. Best for content teams with budget.

$39/mo Visit Jasper →
4

Writesonic

GPT-4-powered writing for blogs, ads, and landing pages. Faster than Jasper for drafting, cheaper for solo creators. Best for startup marketers.

Free / $16/mo Visit Writesonic →
5

Rytr

25+ writing tones, 40+ use cases, and a paragraph rewriter that keeps your meaning intact. Cheapest option in this list by a wide margin. Best for freelancers and high-volume rewriting.

Free / $9/mo Visit Rytr →
6

Copy.ai

Workflow-centric rewriting: build multi-step pipelines that draft, rewrite, and optimize in one flow. Best for teams that need content automation, not just a rewriting button.

Free / $49/mo Visit Copy.ai →

Bottom line: QuillBot is still the fastest, most focused paraphraser for students and researchers. Grammarly wins on grammar depth and tone detection. Jasper and Writesonic beat both for long-form marketing content. Rytr is the budget pick for high-volume rewriting.
